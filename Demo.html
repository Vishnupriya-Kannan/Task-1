Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
He played a crucial role in India's space and missile development programs.
He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
Known as the Missile Man for his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Key Achievements:

  • Led the development of Agni and Prithvi missiles under the IGMDP.
  • Played a pivotal role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Honored with Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

Inspiring Quote:
"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

He passed away on July 28, 2014 July 27, 2015 while giving a lecture in Shillong.
His contribution to ISRO and DRDO is remembered even today.
